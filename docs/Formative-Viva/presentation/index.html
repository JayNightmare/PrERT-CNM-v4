Project Aim and Research Gap

Gap

  • Policy NLP often stops at labels/probabilities.
  • Regulations define obligations but not operational metrics.
  • Stakeholders need measurable, explainable, and auditable risk indicators.

Response

  • Build a four-phase pipeline from standards extraction to validated compliance outputs.
  • Use transformer classification with Bayesian posterior risk integration.
  • Preserve traceability from score to clause to regulation control.
- Existing work often gives labels but not measurable compliance evidence. - Standards frameworks are strong on obligations but weak on operational scoring. - My contribution is the bridge: prediction, uncertainty, and standards traceability in one pipeline.

Problems Encountered and Resolution Status

Problems

  • Class imbalance, especially limited \"system\" examples, caps macro-F1 gains.
  • Risk of misleading model comparisons when splits differ across variants.
  • Auxiliary APP-350 mapping reduced useful label coverage for the current taxonomy.
  • Bayesian posterior can be sensitive to analyst-defined priors.

Resolution / Plan

  • Use focal loss and keep macro metrics as primary decision signals.
  • Enforce policy-level split discipline and non-comparability caveats.
  • Treat APP-350 outcome as negative evidence and keep OPP-115 as benchmark anchor.
  • Schedule prior sensitivity analysis and elicitation workflow in next phase.
- The biggest technical issue is minority-class performance, especially for system. - Methodologically, split discipline and comparability are essential to avoid overstated claims. - Current mitigations are active, and unresolved risks are explicitly in the plan.

Methods for Future Work

  1. Domain-adaptive pretraining and targeted augmentation to improve minority class behaviour.
  2. Compare stronger and long-context transformer families under controlled scaling schedules.
  3. Expand evidence-grounding: retrieval of external behavioural indicators to test policy-application consistency.
  4. Run repeated-seed ablations with fixed splits and leakage controls for defensible claims.
  5. Validate externally on multilingual and cross-jurisdiction policy corpora.
- Future methods are focused on stronger evidence, not only better scores. - I will test architecture, data strategy, and evidence-grounded verification in sequence. - Evaluation will continue to include calibration and uncertainty metrics.

Ethics and Data Governance

  • No human participant recruitment and no direct personal data collection in this pipeline.
  • Data sources are public research corpora and synthetic records under project controls.
  • OPP-115 usage follows research-and-teaching licence constraints noted in project records.
  • Model outputs are probabilistic: they support decisions but do not replace expert legal judgement.
  • Deployment principle: transparent evidence links, uncertainty disclosure, and human oversight before action.
- Ethics stance: low-risk data handling but high responsibility in interpretation and deployment. - Outputs are advisory and should not be treated as legal conclusions. - Transparency and expert oversight are necessary safeguards.
